Yellow Hat is a non-profit volunteer community dedicated to shaping the conversation around technology, policy and digital rights and increasing civic engagement with technology issues.

Digital technology has promised us comfort and ease, but in doing so it has enabled an opaque network of operators to be in control of practices that affect our liberty and rights. The technically complex nature of these practices have kept these issues from the critical lens of the public.

Using the web as a platform, projects from Yellow Hat aim to combine the efficacy of Graphic Design with the interactivity of the web in order to break down the overarching technical processes that govern our everyday lives — such as Net Neutrality, Mass Surveillance, Data Harvesting — into simple visual and interactive explanations.

Yellow Hat hopes to become a resource and a framework for designers, educators, activists, journalists and policy makers who are seeking to raise awareness within the digital arena.

Who are the Hats?

Yellow Hat projects are developed by an open volunteer based community. This community comprises of designers, journalists, developers, politicians, artists, hackers and makers of all kinds who collaborate on projects.

Historically, colored hats have been used to identify different sets of people in the context of computer security. We have had White and Black Hats, Grey and Blue Hats, Red Hats, and now we have Yellow Hats!

Objectives
To Collect

Yellow Hats draw from the myriad of published resources & projects on the web in order to develop a comprehensive reading of an issue. Be it a news article from a leading publication, a personal blog post, or a comment on a YouTube video, or a meme on 4Chan: the Hats connect these varied entities into a single conversation.

To Organize

In a response to the overwhelming amount of information available on the internet on any hot topic, Yellow Hats structure and organize this information into a clear sequence of events that is easy to follow and keep up with.

To Clarify

The Hats draw from this information to develop informational, interactive and participatory experiences. Through this process, the Hats are able to distill and deconstruct any issue into a tiered experience that is valuable to both invested activists and casual browsers.

Values
Balance

Yellow Hat is dedicated to awareness and education. This invokes a responsibility to present all perspectives on any issue. While designing and editing may be inherently political, Yellow Hat as a community should remain apolitical and accommodate different perspectives.

Timing

Yellow Hat must never be isolated from the larger public discourse. Yellow Hat projects should look to capitalize upon popular cultural moments and tropes in an attempt to create an entry into a conversation about techno-politics.

Context

Yellow Hat must use the web browser as a venue for its activities. By occupying a device—agnostic space, Yellow Hat enables universal access, congruent to Yellow Hat’s mission to enable access to techno-political conversations.

Depth

Yellow Hat must recognize and cater to the attention span of the average internet browser. Through its projects and materials, Yellow Hat must be able to communicate to an audience with different levels of enthusiasm.
